Roles

In the game design career, it is common for people to work in big teams, so there are different roles for people to do their own job and contribute their results. Some of the roles/subroles in a team may include:

  • Designer (gameplay, rules, structure)
  • Artist (game art, visuals)
  • Programmer (logic of game, controls the flow)
    • Physics
    • Artificial Intelligence (AI)
    • Graphics
    • User Interface (UI)
    • Network Communications
  • Level Design (creates levels and challenges within game)
  • Sound Engineer (sound effects, sound programming)
  • Tester (assures quality of game before sending the game out)

Salary

Although the career provides a competetive salary, it can usually vary by individual. There are a few factors that can affect the amount of money that a game designer may earn. Some of those factors are:

  • Years of experience
  • Specialty in a specific role
  • Job Title (Senior, Lead)
  • Demand of a job

An average entry-level game designer may earn at least $40,000 and up to $120,000 annually. The average median salary in 2015 for a Lead Game Designer with a Bachelor's Degree was $98,260. In addition, from 2014-2020 there has been a faster than average growth in job outlooks for the career.
